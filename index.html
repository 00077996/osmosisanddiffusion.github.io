What is osmosis?

Osmosis is a process by which molecules of a solvent tend to pass through a semipermeable membrane from a less concentrated solution into a more concentrated one.

Some examples of Osmosis.

  • When you feel thirsty after eating salt.
  • Soaking gummies in water and they expand.
  • Plants taking water into the roots from the surrounding soil.
  • When salt is added to preserve food.
  • Fish absorbing water through their skin and gills.

What is diffusion?

Diffusion is a process by which molecules move from an area of high concentration to an area of low concentration

Some examples of Diffusion.

  1. Food coloring added to water.
  2. Perfume sprayed and can be smelt across the room.
  3. Sugar dissolving without stirring to make water sweeter.
  4. Smoke moving from downstairs to upstairs a house.
  5. Being able to smell when food is cooked.
Differences Between Osmosis and Diffusion
Osmosis Diffusion
Occurs only in liquid Not limited to any state of matter
Requires a semi-permiable membrane Does not require a semi-permiable membrane
Movement of molecules from an area of low concentration to an area of high concentration Movement of molecules from an area of high concentration to an area of low concentration
The flow of molecules in one direction The flow of molecules in all direction
